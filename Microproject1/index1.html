Father of Engineers: Dr. Vishveshwarayya

"A good engineer thinks in reverse and asks himself about the stylistic consequences of the components and their arrangements."

Dr. Mokshagundam Vishveshwarayya (1861–1962), fondly known as the Father of Modern Engineering in India, was born on 15th September 1861 in Muddenahalli, Karnataka. He graduated in civil engineering with distinction from the College of Engineering, Pune, and went on to make remarkable contributions to India’s infrastructure.

He designed and implemented flood protection systems for Hyderabad and constructed the Krishna Raja Sagara (KRS) Dam in Mysore, introducing innovative automatic weir water floodgates.

Serving as the Diwan of Mysore from 1912 to 1918, he actively promoted industrialization, modernization, and economic planning. In recognition of his exceptional contributions, he was awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1955. His vision, dedication, and engineering brilliance continue to inspire generations, and Engineers’ Day in India is celebrated on his birthday, 15th September, in his honor.
